Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our training programs, accreditation, and services.

Is this an accredited qualification? What qualification will I receive from this program?

â–¼

We don't call our program a Certificate since this could be misleading. In Australia, a Certificate is a recognised qualification provided by a Registered Training Organization (RTO). Since psychedelic-assisted therapy is not yet a regulated field with established training standards, there are currently no RTO-recognised certificates available in the practical application and know-how of this specialty.

Current Regulatory Status:

  • No official body accrediting psychedelic-assisted therapy training in Australia
  • No professional organisation currently regulates psychedelic therapy training
  • No program in Australia can offer an official or 'accredited' qualification in psychedelic therapy

Important Distinction:

CPD (Continuing Professional Development) accreditation is not the same as an official qualification or accreditation to deliver psychedelic-assisted therapy in Australia. Some programs advertise CPD accreditation, but this relates to professional development points rather than qualification to practice in this field.

What IS Accepted:

  • TGA Guidelines: Treating teams need a lead therapist with psychedelic therapist training
  • Our Graduates: Currently working as psychedelic therapists in treating teams alongside Authorised Prescribers regulated by the TGA
  • Recognition: Training accepted in clinical trials and private practice settings

What We Provide:

  • Certificate of completion
  • High-quality, evidence-based training from highly experienced practitioners
  • Focus on practical skills, ethics, and safety
  • Training recognized and accepted in clinical trials and private practice

Our Position:

When the field is unregulated and no official qualification exists, in-person instructor experience and training quality matter far more than terminology. We encourage you to evaluate any program based on these factors rather than the language used to describe credentials.

What's the difference between CPD hours and a professional qualification?

â–¼

CPD (Continuing Professional Development) Hours: Recognition points toward maintaining your professional registration (e.g., with APS or AHPRA). Our 35 CPD hours satisfy these requirements.

Professional Qualification: A formal credential certifying you to practice a specific role. Since psychedelic-assisted therapy is not yet officially regulated, no program can offer an "official" qualification. This applies to every training in Australia.

Why This Matters:

  • CPD hours help you maintain professional registration
  • They do NOT constitute a qualification to practice a specialty
  • You still need your base professional registration (psychology, psychiatry, counselling, etc.)
  • PIA training prepares you to practice psychedelic-assisted therapy within your existing professional scope

Who can prescribe psilocybin and MDMA in Australia?

â–¼

Only psychiatrists who have been approved as Authorised Prescribers by the TGA can prescribe psilocybin and MDMA.

Key Points:

  • Psychiatrists must apply and be approved by the TGA
  • They must be approved by a Human Research Ethics Committee (HREC)
  • Treatment must occur in a controlled clinical environment
  • Therapists work as part of the treating team but do NOT prescribe

The Role of Therapists:

Mental health professionals (psychologists, counsellors, social workers, mental health nurses) work alongside the Authorised Prescriber as part of the treating team. Your role and responsibilities are determined by your professional qualifications and the clinical governance framework.

Do I need to be a psychologist to do psychedelic-assisted therapy?

â–¼

No. Treating teams typically include mental health professionals such as:

  • Psychologists
  • Psychiatrists
  • Counsellors
  • Social workers
  • Mental health nurses

Your Base Qualification Determines Your Scope:

Your existing professional qualification (psychology, counselling, nursing, social work, etc.) defines what you can do within a treating team. PIA training prepares you to add psychedelic-assisted therapy skills to your existing professional practice.

What You Need:

  • Existing registration with a regulatory body (AHPRA, PACFA, ACA, etc.)
  • Professional experience in your field (typically 3+ years)
  • Completion of PIA training
  • Work within a treating team with an Authorised Prescriber

How long does psychedelic therapy training take?

PIA's Approach:

  • 12-week online course: Foundational theoretical knowledge
  • 5-day intensive: Hands-on clinical skills training in Byron Bay

Completion of a 5-day intensive does NOT make you highly competent. Competence requires ongoing clinical experience, supervision, and reflective practice beyond the training. The training goal is to equip you with foundational clinical skills and protocol knowledge.
