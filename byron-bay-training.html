Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this an officially accredited qualification?

‚ñº

This is an important distinction: In Australia, a "Certificate" specifically means a recognized qualification from an RTO (Registered Training Organization). Since psychedelic-assisted therapy is not yet a regulated field with established standards, there is currently no RTO-certified qualification available in Australia or internationally.

What we provide: A certificate of completion and high-quality, evidence-based training recognized by the TGA and accepted in clinical trials and private practice. Our graduates are working as psychedelic therapists in treating teams alongside TGA-regulated Authorized Prescribers.

What matters more: In-person instructor experience, training quality, and demonstrated competence‚Äînot terminology. When evaluating any psychedelic training, focus on the credentials of the faculty, their direct clinical trial experience, and their ongoing practice.

What's the difference between CPD hours and professional qualification?

‚ñº

CPD (Continuing Professional Development) hours are recognition points toward maintaining your professional registration (e.g., with APS or AHPRA). Our 35 CPD hours satisfy these requirements.

A professional qualification is a formal credential certifying you to practice a specific role. Since psychedelic-assisted therapy is not yet officially regulated, no program can offer an "official" qualification‚Äîincluding ours. This applies to every training in Australia.

Will I be able to practice immediately after?

‚ñº

This 5-day intensive prepares you to take your first clinical steps as a psychedelic-assisted therapist. Competence, however, requires ongoing clinical experience, supervision, and reflective practice beyond the training.

The training goal is to equip you with foundational clinical skills, protocols, and safety knowledge. You'll need clinical supervision and a commitment to continued learning as you integrate this modality into your practice.
