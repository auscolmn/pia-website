Course Details

Deep dive into each offering

Part A: Understanding Psychedelics

Our online foundational course develops essential knowledge in psychedelic pharmacology, history, and therapeutic frameworks. This course is approved for 4 CPD hours with the Australian Psychological Society.

Completion of Part A unlocks discounted pricing for Part B and demonstrates genuine commitment to evidence-based practice. It's designed for both clinicians new to psychedelics and experienced therapists wanting to formalise their knowledge.

What You'll Learn

  • Module 1: Psychedelic history from indigenous use to modern research
  • Module 2: Pharmacology of psilocybin, MDMA, and other compounds
  • Module 3: Therapeutic mechanisms & evidence-based frameworks
  • Module 4: Australian regulations & ethical considerations
  • Module 5: Integration protocols & client support strategies

Part B: 5-Day Clinical Skills Intensive

Our flagship in-person intensive transforms knowledge into clinical confidence. Led by published researchers and experienced clinicians, this 5-day immersion in Byron Bay covers the complete delivery cycle of MDMA and psilocybin-assisted therapy.

Limited to 25 participants to ensure high-quality mentorship. Target audience: Licensed clinicians, psychologists, psychiatrists, social workers, and assistant facilitators with appropriate credentials.

Daily Breakdown

  • Day 1: MDMA-assisted therapy protocols & preparation techniques
  • Day 2: Session facilitation, presence, and therapeutic presence
  • Day 3: Psilocybin protocols & advanced clinical scenarios
  • Day 4: Safety, crisis management, and challenging presentations
  • Day 5: Integration, follow-up care, and building your practice

Common Questions

FAQ

What are the prerequisites for Part B? ‚ñº

Part B is designed for licensed mental health professionals including psychologists, psychiatrists, counsellors, social workers, and other clinicians with appropriate credentials. While completion of Part A is recommended, it's not mandatory if you have equivalent psychedelic knowledge. Applications are assessed on a case-by-case basis.

Do I need to complete Part A before Part B? ‚ñº

Not required, but strongly recommended. Part A covers foundational concepts that deepen your engagement with Part B. Completing Part A also unlocks pricing discounts for the intensive. If you already have solid psychedelic knowledge from other training, you can apply directly to Part B.

What's included in the Part B accommodation? ‚ñº

Part B includes 5 nights accommodation in Byron Bay and all meals during the training. Accommodation is arranged in comfortable, close-by facilities to foster community. The price does not include flights or transport to Byron Bay.

Are the CPD hours recognised? ‚ñº

Yes. Part A provides 4 hours of APS-approved CPD. Part B provides 35 hours of accredited CPD recognised by the Australian Psychological Society, Australian Counselling Association, and other regulatory bodies. Documentation is provided upon completion.

What if I can't attend all 5 days? ‚ñº

We require full attendance for the complete 5-day intensive. Each day builds on previous content, and the training is designed as an integrated experience. Partial attendance is not available, as it compromises both your learning and group dynamics.

Will there be ongoing mentorship after the course? ‚ñº

Yes. Part B graduates gain access to our community forum, regular case consultation calls, and optional follow-up mentorship sessions. We support your transition into clinical practice with real-world guidance and peer connection.

What are the payment and refund policies? ‚ñº

A 50% deposit secures your place. Full payment is due 30 days before the training. We offer a full refund if you withdraw more than 60 days before the course. Withdrawals within 60 days forfeit the deposit. Transfers to future cohorts are available within 90 days.

Can I use Part A as part of my own training program? ‚ñº

Yes. Part A is flexible and designed to fit into your schedule. You'll have lifetime access to all materials, so you can reference them during and after the course. Many clinicians recommend Part A to their own supervisees and trainees.

Do you offer international participants? ‚ñº

Absolutely. Part B welcomes international clinicians. We have therapists from New Zealand, Singapore, and beyond. Byron Bay is accessible via Melbourne or Brisbane airports. Part A is entirely online, so it's available globally.
