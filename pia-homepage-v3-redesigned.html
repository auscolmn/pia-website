About PIA

A values-driven organisation at the forefront of psychedelic therapy

Psychedelic Institute Australia offers the highest quality psychedelic therapy training and education to therapists and clinicians across Australia. We are led by published researchers and clinicians with direct experience in psychedelic clinical trials.

Our mission is to ensure the safe, ethical, and effective integration of psychedelic-assisted therapy into Australian healthcare — grounded in evidence, guided by integrity.

Evidence-Based Clinician-Led Ethics-First Trial-Trusted

Your Training Pathway

From foundational knowledge to clinical practice

Our structured pathway takes you from understanding psychedelic therapy to delivering it with confidence in real-world settings.

01

Foundational Knowledge

Begin with our online course developed with Psychedelics Today. Build your understanding of psychedelic pharmacology, history, therapeutic frameworks, and the Australian regulatory landscape.

Online · Self-Paced
02

Clinical Skills Intensive

A 5-day immersive training in Byron Bay. Learn psilocybin and MDMA-assisted protocols, screening, safety management, and integration techniques from Australia's leading clinicians.

In-Person · Byron Bay
03

Research Team Training

Specialised training for universities, CROs, and clinical trial teams. We prepare therapist teams to deliver psychedelic-assisted therapy within research protocols to the highest standards.

Customised · Teams
04

Consultancy & Support

Ongoing guidance for individuals, organisations, and businesses exploring psychedelic therapy — from regulatory navigation to clinical implementation and practice development.

Ongoing · Flexible

Our Approach

Evidence-based, experiential, and deeply human

We bridge rigorous science with the relational, embodied nature of psychedelic therapy.

Research-Grounded

Built on clinical trial evidence, published research, and validated protocols including the MAPS manual for MDMA-assisted psychotherapy.

Safety & Ethics First

Every aspect prioritises participant safety, informed consent, cultural sensitivity, and the ethical responsibilities of consciousness-expanding work.

Experiential Learning

Intimate groups in Byron Bay foster deep relational learning with a high trainer-to-participant ratio and direct mentorship from expert clinicians.
